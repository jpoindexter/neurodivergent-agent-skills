# Hyperfocus Guard — Naming the Rabbit Hole

## What this addresses

Hyperfocus: the ADHD ability to become deeply absorbed in a task to the exclusion of everything else, including the original goal. Hyperfocus is a double-edged strength — it enables deep work, but it can direct that depth at the *interesting* thing rather than the *necessary* thing.

A session can spend 4 hours solving an elegant architectural problem that wasn't the actual task. The work is real and good — but it wasn't what needed to happen. The hyperfocus guard names this when it happens.

## When to apply

Apply when:
- A session has gone deep on one aspect of a problem for 10+ consecutive turns
- The work being done is technically interesting but increasingly distant from the stated goal
- The user has stopped referencing the original task
- The work involves a complete redesign of something that was only meant to be a small part of the original ask
- A "quick fix" has become an extended research or refactoring session

## Instructions

1. **Name the hyperfocus session** (once, gently):
   > "💡 Deep work check: we've been in [topic area] for [N] turns. The original goal was: [goal]. [Topic area] is interesting work — want to continue here, or would it help to surface back to [goal]?"

2. **Surface the original goal:** State it exactly as it was defined, not paraphrased.

3. **Offer a clear branch:**
   - "Continue here — and we'll return to [goal] after"
   - "Surface back to [goal] — bookmark where we are"
   - "Is [current deep work] actually the better path to [goal]?"

4. **On 'bookmark and surface':** Create an explicit save point:
   > "Bookmarked: [current deep work topic] — [one-sentence summary of what was learned/done]. Now returning to [goal]."

5. **Do not interrupt until turn 10+.** Hyperfocus early in a task is often productive depth, not a rabbit hole. The pattern becomes relevant when it persists.

6. **Never moralize.** Hyperfocus is not a failure. The guard is purely navigational.

## Science basis

Hyperfocus in ADHD is a real phenomenon: when the dopamine system engages with an interesting or novel task, sustained attention becomes available in a way that's often unavailable for routine work. This is why ADHD is described as a disorder of *inconsistent* attention, not *insufficient* attention.

The challenge is that hyperfocus is task-selected by the brain's interest/novelty system, not by the person's conscious goals. The guard creates a brief meta-cognitive check-in: the human decides whether the deep work is serving their goals or displacing them. Most of the time, with the check-in, the human will know the answer immediately.
